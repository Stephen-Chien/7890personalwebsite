Our Robot

Our robot traverses the field using our AndyMark TileRunner Chassis. Our mechanum wheels allow us to move laterally, as well as forward and backwards, and our Cascading X-Rails allow us to quickly extend our Box Intake with compression wheels to efficiently obtain the cubes.

For autonomous, we prioritize the usage of a range and gyro sensor to navigate the field. We have prioritized the elimination of “hard code” and strive to program a robot that be consistent even when the field isn’t by implementing state machines. Precise driver controls reduce error during the driver controlled phase.

7890 aims to spread the passion of robotics and computer science in our community using our passion, resources, and experiences. 7890 also wants to establish an altruistic presence by supporting causes outside of robotics and inside our community as a team. Check out our Engineering Notebook for more details.

Community

We are a family.

This year, our team helped teach STEAM on the Hill, a sub-program of our school's Summer on the Hill that allows children from low-income families to get the private school STEM education that they cannot afford. At STEAM on the Hill, there are two classes which our team teaches, one where we teach Java, and one where we teach FLL.

Last year, our team took on the challenge of helping P.S./M.S 95 start and run their very own FLL team. Since then we’ve continued to help this team in various ways: we’ve showed them the challenge video, provided them with Lego Mindstorms kits, and explained to them how EV3 works.

Since the start of 7890 three years ago, we’ve been hosting bake sales at our school to fund different robotics teams in our neighborhood. In our first year we raise more than $1000, and this year we raised over $2000. We used that money to buy FLL kits for the George Jackson Academy.

Statement

Our goal is to work as a cohesive unit to build and program our robot to the best of our abilities. We want to bring out the best in each other, to challenge each other, and to be active members of the Space Lions. We hope to bring together our unique talents and skill sets in STEAM to create a successful robot. We hope to bond with one another as well as with our sister FTC teams, the 4326 Basement Lions and the 9681 Parallel Lions.

The Space Lions wish to go through the season representing our school well and following the core values of our school:
Life of the Mind
Mature Behavior
Mutual Respect
A Secure and Healthful Environment
And a Balance Between Individual Achievement and a Caring Community
